O Perfection

O Perfection, I've found my life in your station— a single thought destroys me, a moment from you in separation. I've affixed myself in prayer of your presence; in every breath, I whisper your sweet name of intoxication. God knows my heart’s condition— not broken, but burning with the fire of your remembrance. O beloved, my soul is stationed in your nearness. Intoxicate me more with the perfectest of your smiles. I do not seek escape from this longing— I seek only to dwell in it, to be undone by it, to be made whole by it. Your absence is not emptiness— it is the echo of your presence ringing louder than silence. I do not love you in halves. I love you in the way a flame loves the air— consumed, but never regretful. If I am ruined, let it be by your beauty. If I am lost, let it be in your direction. O perfection, you are not a dream I chase— you are the prayer I live. And if I never reach you, then let my reaching be my devotion.
by : Muhammed Dastigeer Khan
